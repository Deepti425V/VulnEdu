What is NVD & MITRE?

NVD (National Vulnerability Database) and MITRE are the two foundational organizations that maintain the vulnerability intelligence systems used worldwide. Understanding their roles helps you navigate the cybersecurity landscape more effectively.

üèõÔ∏è National Vulnerability Database (NVD)

Who: Managed by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), part of the U.S. Department of Commerce
What: The U.S. government's repository of standards-based vulnerability management data
Why it matters: Provides comprehensive vulnerability information with standardized severity scores (CVSS)

What NVD Provides:

  • CVSS Scores: Standardized severity ratings for vulnerabilities
  • CPE Names: Standardized naming for IT products and platforms
  • CWE Mappings: Links vulnerabilities to weakness categories
  • Reference Links: Technical details, patches, and advisories
  • API Access: Machine-readable data feeds for automation
Real-World Usage:
Security teams use NVD data to prioritize patching, vulnerability scanners reference NVD for severity scores, and compliance frameworks often require NVD-based vulnerability management.

üî¨ MITRE Corporation

Who: Non-profit organization that operates federally funded research and development centers
What: Creates and maintains the CVE and CWE programs
Why it matters: Provides the universal naming conventions that make vulnerability communication possible

MITRE's Key Programs:

CVE Program
Assigns unique identifiers to publicly known vulnerabilities (CVE-YYYY-NNNNN format)
CWE Program
Catalogs common types of software and hardware weaknesses
ATT&CK Framework
Knowledge base of adversary tactics and techniques based on real-world observations
Global Impact:
MITRE's standards are used by security vendors, researchers, and organizations worldwide to ensure consistent vulnerability identification and communication.
